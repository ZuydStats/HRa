Week 4 - Workshop

Workshop (HRA3) - People Power Planning

What you will learn

  • You know how to make an informed decision based on data and data analysis.
  • You know how to set up data analysis for an HR plan for the expansion of a hotel.
  • You are familiar with going beyond the data in your data set and using it to adapt to future changes.

What you will need to prepare

  • Re-read the case study of the Myriad Hotel, specifically on the topic of power planning.
  • Go over the material on quantitative staffing that you have studied with your HR-plan group.

Your in-class materials

  • You read the case study of the Myriad Hotel in week 2 (see Moodle). Re-read this text and keep in mind that the capacity of the Myriad Hotel will double because of the expansion (see top of page 4).
  • Charles suggests to "double the number of staff in rooms and F&B departments", while Stefan wants to do things differently, based on the data.
  • What would you do?
  • You can try some of the following analyses:
  • Not all staff might be equally productive. See which staff has the lowest productivity (in quality/quantity).
  • Check the relation between productivity and salary (1.0 FTE, transform/calculate; set FTE to continuous) by making a scatterplot.
  • Use filters to look at which managers are delivering the lowest quality.
  • Next to salary, qualification level might be a factor. Use a box plot to check whether higher-qualified employees are more reliable performers, and/or are less absent in their work.
  • The labour market is very tight. Maybe current employees working part-time (0.5 FTE) can be tempted to work more hours. Use a clustered bar chart to check whether women work part-time more often than men (for this to work, consider changing the variable FTE to a more appropriate type). Follow up with a chi-square test to check whether any difference you found is actually significant.
  • Combine these results with your preparation and prior knowledge on staffing to deliver your advice.
