What does this course look like?

Twice a week, you will participate in our flipped-classroom classes. These sessions are 60 minutes and are labelled as 5HRA1 and 5HRA2.

What does the exam look like?

The exam consists of 60 multiple-choice questions with four answer options each. Your score is determined using relative scoring (Cohen–Schotanus method), and a correction for guessing is applied.

The Cohen–Schotanus method means that your grade is determined in relation to the performance of the whole group, using the highest score achieved as a reference point. This is beneficial because it protects you against an exam that turns out to be more difficult than expected. If the test is harder for everyone, the grading scale adjusts accordingly, ensuring fairness. Your result therefore reflects how well you performed compared to your peers under the same conditions, rather than being strictly tied to a fixed percentage of correct answers.

In addition, a guessing correction is applied because each question has four options. Since random guessing gives a 25% chance of being correct, the scoring system compensates for this.

To get a concrete look at what the exam questions looks like have a look at the formative test
