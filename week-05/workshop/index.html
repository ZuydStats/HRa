Week 5 - Workshop - Satisfaction

What you will learn

  • You know how to make an informed decision based on data and data analysis.
  • More precisely, you can analyze which group of employees is unhappy/happy and about which aspects of their job.
  • You are able to explore and analyze various variables of your choice using graphs and tests to arrive at a conclusion.

What you will need to prepare

  • Re-read the case study of the Myriad Hotel, specifically on the topic of staff satisfaction.
  • Remember that you read the case study of the Myriad Hotel in week 1 (see Moodle). Now study the case in more detail and pay special attention to the bold highlighted elements that could be important for employee satisfaction.
  • Consider all variables in the dataset that might be relevant to answer this question: Are all Myriad employees happy with the current employment situation (or parts of it) at the Myriad Hotel?
  • Note that the case study text can give valuable hints about where to start and focus your analysis.
  • Specifically, look into the relations between:
    • Staff satisfaction with salary and their actual salary, based on 1.0 FTE (tip: compute).
    • Staff satisfaction with relations at work and their seniority in the hotel.
    • Staff satisfaction with growth opportunities and their number of training days.
    • Staff satisfaction with recognition at work and gender.
    • Staff satisfaction with salary, relations at work, recognition, and growth for managers versus operatives.
    • Cut-off point: staff is in management if salary > EUR 2,500 per month (tip: transform).
    • Bonus: check no. 1 again on salary, now separately for managers and operatives. Try to explain.
